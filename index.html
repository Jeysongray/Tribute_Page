The life of Ben Carson

  • 1951- In the year of our Lord, an amazing human was born, Benjamin Solomon Carson to two simple humans in Detroit Michigan.

  • 1984- He was the Director of Pediatric Neurosurgery at Johns Hopkins Hospital in Maryland from 1984 until his retirement in 2013.

  • As a Pioneer neurosugeon, Carson's achievements include performing the only successful separation of conjoined twins joined at the back of the head.

  • He became the youngest chief of pediatric neurosurgery in the country at age 33 and has received more than 60 honorary doctorate degrees.

  • 2008- He was bestowed the Presidential Medal of Freedom, the highest civilian award in the United States for his exceptional work in the field of neurosurgery.

  • 2015- He announced he was running for the Republican nomination in the 2016 presidential election at a rally in his hometown of Detroit

  • 2016- Following the Super Tuesday primaries, he suspended his campaign and announced he would be the new national chairman of My Faith Votes, a group that encourages Christians to exercise their civic duty to vote.
